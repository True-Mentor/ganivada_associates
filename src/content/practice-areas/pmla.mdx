  - q: What is the PMLA and when does it apply?
    a: The Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 applies to scheduled offences and deals with attachment of property, prosecution for money laundering, and obligations on reporting entities. It is enforced by the Enforcement Directorate (ED).
  - q: Can the ED attach my property under PMLA without a court order?
    a: The ED can provisionally attach property for up to 180 days under Section 5 PMLA pending adjudication by the Adjudicating Authority. We advise on challenging provisional attachments and appearing before the Adjudicating Authority.
  - q: Do you represent both accused persons and complainants in financial crime matters?
    a: We primarily advise on the defence side: advising persons who are the subject of ED inquiries, PMLA attachment, or prosecution.

## PMLA & Financial Law Practice

We advise individuals and entities on matters arising under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 and related financial legislation.

## How We Help

- **PMLA Defence**: Advising persons under ED inquiry, appearing before the Adjudicating Authority, and representing accused in PMLA prosecution before the Special Court.
- **Challenging Attachments**: Filing applications before the Adjudicating Authority and the Appellate Tribunal for PMLA (ATPMLA) against provisional and confirmed attachments.
- **Bail in PMLA Cases**: Advising on and arguing bail applications in PMLA cases (noting the twin conditions under Section 45 PMLA).
- **Compliance Advisory**: Advising reporting entities (financial institutions, real estate agents, etc.) on their Know Your Customer (KYC), Customer Due Diligence (CDD), and suspicious transaction reporting obligations.
- **Bank Fraud Matters**: Advising on proceedings before the Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT) and Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunal (DRAT).

## Typical Matters

- Challenging provisional attachments of property by the Enforcement Directorate
- Bail applications in PMLA scheduled offence cases
- Appearances before the Adjudicating Authority and ATPMLA
- Compliance advice for businesses subject to PMLA obligations
- Bank recovery suits before DRTs in Hyderabad
